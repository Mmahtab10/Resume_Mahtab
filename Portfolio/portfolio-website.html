Distributix (Distributed Ticketing System)

AWS Python Next.js Typescript

Have you ever eagerly waited for Taylor Swift or Drake’s concert tickets and the ticketing system crashed for you? Eventbrite and Ticketmasters being the monopoly in the ticketing industry was off putting to many. This meant they could get away with high latency, high ticket prices, while offering less innovative solutions. Here comes DistribuTix aiming to solve the challenge. DistribuTix is a robust, scalable, and reliable distributed ticketing system designed to transform the traditional ticketing landscape by leveraging advanced distributed systems principles. This project, developed as part of the CPSC 559 course (Introduction to Distributed Systems) at the University of Calgary, aims to address the complexities and inefficiencies inherent in conventional ticketing frameworks.

Predicting Income Level Based on Census Data

Python Pandas Scikit-learn Mglearn

This project, "Predicting Income Levels," utilizes the U.S. census "Adult" dataset to predict whether an individual earns more than $50,000 annually using machine learning techniques. The project compares three machine learning models—Logistic Regression, KNeighborsClassifier, and SVC—evaluating their effectiveness for binary classification. The approach involves preprocessing the data (handling missing values, encoding categorical data, and scaling features), creating pipelines for streamlined experimentation, and using metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score for model evaluation. The goal is to provide insights into income determinants through rigorous data analysis and modeling, making it useful for socioeconomic studies and policymaking.
